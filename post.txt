“Oh, I do.” The Asian chick next to me stands. “I may need to see one of you soon.” She replies in near-perfect English.

“Better discuss the price now, then. Need help with that suitcase?”

“The grey Civic, I assume?" She stood, leaving the humongous suitcase by my side. "As for the pay, you shall know once I get there.”

-0-

Getaway driving is a straightforward job. You receive the time and location, drive them to the destination, and get your cut of whatever dirty deeds you helped them get their asses out from. Questions can bury a nine-millimeter in your brain once they step out of the car.

“... Sorry.”

Doesn’t mean questions wouldn’t arise, though.

“’Tis fine. Any human would have thought I was mendacious and resorted to... such a course of action as yours.”

Especially when “Mr. Huang and his pet fox” turned out to be a petite with luggage half her size.

“That explains not ripping off my throat?”

No question, indeed; once the neon lights of the city gave way to the full moon, survival was a gurgling beg for mercy with her canines sinking into your neck, a payback for having pressed your .38 against hers’.

“Heh. More a timely save from this gentleman.” There is a small thud against the corpse lying on the car floor. “Had he not threatened to chop off my tails, his neck wouldn’t be half as delicious as yours’. Better thank him properly.”

“My deepest gratitude to you, kind miss.” The lack of response makes me awkwardly patched in another “sorry,” the third time I tried to smooth out the situation.

“Enough with the palliatives.” The mellifluous voice behind me tints of mockery. “Out of one-liners for socializing, Mr. Taxiturn?”

“Don’t really need ‘em, actually. Better shut my trap and endure a bit of curiosity than opening another asshole on my head.”

Silence, then a pair of cresents glistens through the rearview mirror.

“Isn’t this curiosity too great to bear, though?” She grins. “Talk, or risk my fractiousness.”

I sigh. Not knowing what “fractiousness” even means, I decide not to risk becoming another body on its way to the landfill.

“Weren’t you some kind of… wanderer or something? Why not lay low and avoid the Triads?”

Through the mirror, I see the fox in question turns to the window. Moonlight streaks down her neck, pooling up behind the collarbones.

“I needed to find a friend. One of my kind.” Her gaze flicks back to me. “And the word is ‘wanderlust’.”

“There’re two of you? ...What’s funny?”

“Ah-ha-ha! Who do you humans think had reigned over these forests before you razed them to the ground and built your unsightly temples in their stead?”

“Going hobo ‘cause the Lord took over your business?” Before I can worry about being bled to death for my untimely joke, she answers.

“Heh, but no. It was my decision to leave.”

“Why?” My retort comes immediately. A worshipped existence with every wish catered for by the blindly devouted? I would trade my right arm for that.

“You should not.” In the dark, the amber eyes seem to burn holes into my seat. “Power is the throne you are chained to.”

“Did you just read my mind? And what was that Plato shit supposed to mean anyway?”

“I guess you do suffer from a paucity of wisdom, after all. Such is the nature of the immature.” She sighs and flops down the seats. “Tell me, what does this job bring you?”

“Huh?” The surprise question takes me a while to reply. “Booze, bitches, adrenaline. I could go on.”

“All that boon, hmm?” She asks. “At what cost?”

The shadows of pine trees spill onto the asphalt, lining the poorly-lit highway with grotesque pagoda-like silhouettes. My first time seeing one was on a tattered brochure advertizing Kyoto summer trips on Dad’s working desk way back when.

“…That city’s the back of my hand. Spent my whole life learning every sideroad, every parts dealer, every safe house. Can’t do that again elsewhere...” I trail off. The throne you are chained to, eh?

“Once, there was a maiden, who became wise enough to realize she outlived everything around her. And once they realized it too, her likeness were crafted, built, and burned, again and again for millennia." The fox's voice cracks a little, and she pauses. "She wanted retirement from godhood, but was unfortunately blinded by its radiance. Of her kin, she was the last to realize the fallacy of her existence. That she was no more than a rock her worshippers deemed divine. Do you know what she did after that?”

Without understanding half of what she just said, I reply with a “What?”

She chuckles.

“She tried to live dangerously.”

“Nice doin’ business with ye, mate.” The old man’s smile is filled with crooked teeth. He slaps the side of the industrial-grade garbage shredder. “Five minutes and them boys'll be no different from Campbells.”

“Mhmm.” I nod and leave. Isn’t long before his call turns my head.

“Haven’t seen that chink ‘round.” He says. “What’s the goin’ rate?”

“Watch y’mouth. She’s a business acquaintance.”

Ignoring the man, I reach for the car, at the same time as her on the other side.

“Business?” She giggles.

“We’re chasing after your pal to Vegas. Know how much the gas’ gonna cost?”

“Certainly not as much as having a fair maiden riding shotgun.”

"Is this your idea of pay?"

I laugh in defeat and turn the key. Something in me changed tonight, transmuted, like the maiden beside me when she leaped from my throat to the dumbstruck gangster whose cleaver went frozen in his hand, her nine tails floating behind like fiery blossom petals. And it makes me want to live dangerously.
